What's here

Phuket is a small island with a very big story. People have been arriving here for more than a thousand years — by sea, by river, and lately by plane — and each group has left a mark you can still find today.

How to use this book

Words with a dotted red line under them are clickable — tap or click to see a short definition. Each piece also has a map or visual you can interact with. The book works on a phone or a laptop.
A Thousand Years of Arrivals
An interactive map of eight waves of migration into Phuket — Sea Gypsies, Tamil traders, Malay settlers, Hokkien tin miners, and more. Click any route to read its story; hover the city dots to see what each place is.

The Heroines
1785: a Burmese army surrounds Thalang, the old capital of the island. The governor has just died. Two sisters — Chan and Mook — organise the defence, and the king of Siam gives them a title almost no woman had ever held.

The Tin Island
How a soft, silvery metal in Phuket's rivers and seabed shaped the island — from small Malay miners to giant dredge ships, then collapse, then tourism.

Historical Fiction · Chapters 1–4
Phuket Town, 1901. Mei is fourteen, the daughter of a Hokkien tin merchant. A runaway mine worker hides in her family's storage room. A British engineer arrives with a machine that can do the work of fifty men. Each chapter ends with a short author's note about the real history behind it.
